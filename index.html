Jamaican Banana Fritters

"This recipe is a homage to my culture and my long-lasting sweet tooth. Try making this at home!"

Ingredients

  • 4 large super ripe bananas (or 1 ½ cups mashed)
  • ¾ cup Whole Wheat flour
  • ¾ to 1 cup All-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 2–4 tablespoons brown sugar (adjust to sweetness preference)
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt (or adjust to taste)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  1. Mash the bananas until they become a chunky paste.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In one bowl, combine the flours, baking powder, sugar, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. Mix wet ingredients: In a separate small bowl, mix the vanilla (and any other liquids if using).
  4. Combine: Pour the vanilla/wet ingredients into the mashed banana mixture.
  5. Incorporate flour: Slowly add the dry mix to the banana mix. Do this a little at a time, stirring in between additions until fully incorporated. (Do not overmix).
  6. Heat oil: Add oil to a pan or skillet on medium heat.
  7. Fry: Once the oil is hot, spoon the mixture into small dollops (about the size of a mini pancake).
  8. Flip: Once the edges are bubbling and golden, flip to cook on the other side.
  9. Drain: After they are done frying, remove them from the pan and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  10. Let cool slightly and enjoy! :)
